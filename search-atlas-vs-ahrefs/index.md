## Overview of Search Atlas vs Ahrefs

Search Atlas positions itself as a next-generation AI SEO platform that combines the depth of traditional tools like Ahrefs with powerful automation, content generation, and local SEO features. Ahrefs is known for its strong backlink database and keyword research, but Search Atlas offers significantly more user seats, higher quotas, historical data depth, and integrated AI tools at a better value for agencies.

Search Atlas includes 100 trillion backlinks, 500 million indexed domains, and 5 billion keywords across 200 countries, while providing white-label reporting, multi-user access, and seamless workflows from competitor research to automated execution.

## Key Feature Comparison

Search Atlas outperforms Ahrefs in several critical areas for modern SEO teams:

- **Agency-Focused Design**: Search Atlas includes multiple user seats in every plan (Pro includes 5 seats) and full white-label dashboards. Ahrefs limits most plans to 1 seat with extra charges for additional users.
- **Unlimited Projects and Higher Quotas**: Search Atlas supports unlimited projects on higher plans with generous crawl and tracking limits. Ahrefs restricts projects and keywords more tightly.
- **Content and AI Capabilities**: Search Atlas features [Content Genius](/search-atlas/content-genius), [SEO topical maps](/search-atlas/seo-topical-maps), bulk AI content generation, and [OTTO SEO AI](/search-atlas/otto-seo-ai) automation. Ahrefs lacks comparable native AI content tools.
- **Local SEO Tools**: Search Atlas offers heatmaps, geo grids, citation building, and [Google Business Profile Galactic](/search-atlas/google-business-profile-galactic) integration. Ahrefs has limited local SEO depth.
- **Reporting and Tracking**: Search Atlas provides custom report builder with GSC/GA integration and publicly shareable reports. Ahrefs reporting is more limited.
